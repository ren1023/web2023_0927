1. Key Partners

Identify the external organizations, suppliers, or partners that you will collaborate with to achieve your business goals and deliver value.

2. Key Activities

Describe the key activities your business needs to perform to create and deliver value to customers. This could include manufacturing, marketing, research, etc.

3. Value Propositions

Describe the unique value or benefit that your product or service offers to your customers. What problem does it solve or need does it fulfill?

4. Customer Relationships

Explain the type of relationships you intend to establish and maintain with your customers. This could be through personal assistance, self-service, automated systems, etc.

5. Customer Segments

Identify the different groups of people or organizations that your business aims to serve. These are your target customers.

6. Key Resources

List the critical assets, infrastructure, and resources required to deliver your value proposition and operate your business effectively. This might include technology, physical facilities, staff, etc.

7. Channels

Outline the various channels or methods through which you will reach and communicate with your customers. This could include online sales, retail stores, social media, etc.

8. Cost Structure

Outline the costs and expenses associated with running your business. This includes both fixed and variable costs, such as production costs, marketing expenses, salaries, etc.

9. Revenue Streams

Define the different sources of revenue for your business. How will you generate income? This could include one-time sales, subscription fees, advertising, etc.
